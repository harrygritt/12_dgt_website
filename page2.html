Heavy Hitters

Bobby Fischer was a chess grandmaster and is regarded as one of the greats in Chess. Born in Chicago, Illinois, US on March 9, 1943, the chess legend has had an interesting career. He reached a peak ELO rating of 2785 which is the widely used rating for chess that measures the relative strength and skill of chess players. Fischer held the title for World Champion between 1972-1975. Not only did Fischer contribute to the world of chess with his brilliant play, but he also published a book in 1969, My 60 Memorable Games, whilst also inventing Fischer Random Chess, also known as Chess960. This variant of chess entails the initial positions of peices being randomised to one of 960 possible options.

Unfortunately, Fischer passed away aged 64 on January 17, 2008. This came after his prolific career led him to play chess matches that were frowned upon by the US government given the political situation at the time (Cold Wr and embargo on Yugoslavia). Evuantually, the US government revoked his passport adn Fischer managed to be granted Icelandic citizenship in 2005 where he lived untill his passing away.

Garry Kasparov is another chess grandmaster that can be debated to be the greatest of all time. Kasparov was born on 13 April, 1963 in Baku, Azerbaijan SSR, Soviet Union. He reached a peak ELO rating of 2851 in July 1999 and was a World Champion from 1985 to 2000. He has also been apart of movements that oppose the administration of Vladimir Putin which eventually led to Kasparov leaving Russia and living in New York woth family. In 2014, the chess genius managed to gain Croatian citizenship and now resides in Podstrana near Split.
